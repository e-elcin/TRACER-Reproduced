Section: Ablation Study

5.1 Component Contribution Analysis
To understand which uncertainty components contribute most to failure prediction,
we analyzed both individual component performance and their linear correlations
with task outcomes (Table 1). Our analysis reveals:

• Linear correlations are weak (r < 0.15 for all components)
• Best individual component: Da (AUROC=0.627)

Crucially, weak individual signals do not preclude effective combination:
TRACER's non-linear aggregation (MAX formula) can capture complex patterns
that linear correlations miss, as evidenced by strong combined performance.

5.2 Formulation Variant Comparison
We evaluated four TRACER formulation variants through exhaustive grid search
optimization (Table 2). Key findings:

• MAX variant achieves best performance (AUROC=0.7027)
• Improves over best individual component by +12.1%
• Improves over additive baseline by 4.9%
• Optimal parameters: α=6.0, β=8.0, γ=7.0
• Top-k=15% filtering and ensemble_weight=10%
• Non-zero α validates inquiry drift (Da) importance
• Non-zero β validates agent coherence (Do) importance

This demonstrates that TRACER's value lies not in individual component
strength, but in how the MAX formula strategically combines them.
